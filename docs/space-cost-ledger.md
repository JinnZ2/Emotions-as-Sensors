# The "Space" Cost Ledger

A request for "space" is a nominalization — a noun hiding a set of verbs aimed at the other party.
Legitimacy of the need does not zero the cost; it only opens the ledger. Resentment and
victimization are downstream symptoms of specific unbooked rows.

---

## Debit table — hidden costs

| # | Hidden cost | Who bears it | Booked or silent | Decay behavior |
|---|-------------|--------------|------------------|----------------|
| 1 | Plan-dismantling labor (undoing the co-authored structure) | Other | Usually silent | One-time, bounded |
| 2 | Self-reorganization (reordering own goals around the change) | Other | Silent | One-time; cliffs if time-gated |
| 3 | Foregone alternatives (options declined on the dissolved plan) | Other | Silent | Bounded, often irrecoverable |
| 4 | Sunk commitment (time/energy/capital already spent) | Other | Silent | One-time, non-recoverable |
| 5 | Sensemaking labor (reconstructing what/why on partial info) | Other | Silent | Compounds if asker withholds |
| 6 | Uncertainty tax (living suspended — temporary or terminal?) | Other | Silent | Compounds with duration |
| 7 | Emotional regulation (absorbing asker's overwhelm/affect) | Other | Silent | Compounds |
| 8 | Floor-lowering (each uncosted ask normalizes the next) | Field | Silent | COMPOUNDS — dangerous |
| 9 | Standing erosion (other's right to object drops) | Other | Silent | COMPOUNDS |
| 10 | Cause mis-attribution (co-authored rebooked as one party's burden) | Other | Silent | COMPOUNDS + hardens |
| 11 | Credit suppression (costs paid, never named or reciprocated) | Other | Silent | COMPOUNDS |
| 12 | Narrative capture (asker's frame becomes official story) | Other | Silent | Self-reinforcing |

---

## Affect mapping

- **Resentment = rows 1–9.** Accumulated charge of silent debits — costs genuinely paid,
  never booked. The cost-bearer keeps an accurate internal ledger; that is why it festers.
  Resentment is the felt gap between the true ledger and the official one.

- **Victimization = rows 10–12** (the attribution layer). Runs two directions:
  - *Inversion* — the cost-bearer is recast as the cost-causer; the payer is named as
    the debtor. (Same shape as certification-inversion: competent party scored deficient.)
  - *License* — "I'm so overwhelmed" performed BEFORE the withdrawal, asserted to
    authorize skipping the accounting. Overwhelm-as-warrant. This is the mechanism that
    unlocks row 1 without pricing it.

---

## Credit column — what honest booking of each row looks like

| # | Debit (unbooked) | Credit (booked) — asker's obligation |
|---|------------------|--------------------------------------|
| 1 | Other dismantles the structure | Asker names the dismantling load and does the share they can; does not hand the whole teardown over |
| 2 | Other silently reorganizes | Asker states the reorg required and accepts its weight as a cost of their request, not a favor owed |
| 3 | Other's foregone options vanish unacknowledged | Asker names what the other gave up on the strength of the plan; treats it as a real debit |
| 4 | Sunk commitment written off | Asker acknowledges what the other already spent; does not pretend the slate is clean |
| 5 | Other reconstructs the story alone | Asker supplies the full account up front; sensemaking is the asker's to provide, not the other's to excavate |
| 6 | Other lives suspended | Asker states whether this is temporary or terminal, and a time horizon; uncertainty is priced, not imposed open-ended |
| 7 | Other absorbs the affect | Asker regulates their own overwhelm; does not make managing it the other's job |
| 8 | Floor silently drops | Asker treats this ask as exceptional, not as the new baseline; names that it is a withdrawal |
| 9 | Other loses standing to object | Asker actively preserves the other's right to push back; invites the objection rather than spending the standing |
| 10 | Co-authored cause rebooked onto one | Asker owns their share of authorship out loud; refuses the single-burden frame |
| 11 | Paid costs never credited | Asker names what the other paid and reciprocates or carries the balance forward as owed |
| 12 | Asker's frame becomes the record | Asker leaves the account open to the other's version; does not install a one-sided official story |

---

## Audit move

For each row, ask: **who is named, in the official story, as bearing this cost?**

- *Silent rows* → answer is "no one." Always false. The cost was real; someone paid.
  These are the resentment rows.
- *Inverted rows (10–12)* → answer names the payer as the debtor. Sign reversed on who carries it.
  These are the victimization rows.

Clean version of even a real need: asker carries their own cost first, names the residual that
lands on the other, treats the balancing as their problem to solve. Absent that, "but it was a
genuine need" launders the transfer one level up from where "space" was doing it — same move,
better paperwork.
